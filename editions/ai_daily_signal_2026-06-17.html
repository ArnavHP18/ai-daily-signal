Day 6: Anthropic's best models stay dark — now framed as a US policy reversal, with Bessent and Hegseth at the table

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain disabled worldwide into Wednesday, six days after the Jun 12 US Commerce directive barring foreign-national access. Monday's in-person D.C. talks produced no agreement and no restoration date, and the cast is now clearer: reporting puts Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth among the officials shaping the response (Foreign Policy), while Bloomberg frames the whole episode as a reversal of US AI policy — Washington moving from promotion to restriction. The contradiction is sharpening: even as the export order keeps the models offline, the White House is reportedly weighing expanded Mythos access across federal agencies. The trigger remains Amazon's jailbreak report plus calls from ≥5 firms; the China-access concern stays uncorroborated. Still no clock.

So what: this is now a six-day negotiation with cabinet-level principals and no deadline — the government is simultaneously banning the model for the public and shopping it for itself. Keep your fallback live and price a real migration; do not bet on a quick restoration.

Where it stands. As of this Wednesday scan, both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are still offline for all users worldwide — Day 6 of the blackout; every other Claude model remains available, with traffic routed to Opus 4.8. Anthropic has not announced a restoration date and continues to dispute the directive while complying (Fortune, Anthropic).

The negotiation. Anthropic flew senior technical staff to Washington for the first face-to-face session since the order; per CNBC the talks are framed as deal-seeking, but no agreement has been reached. New in the reporting: Foreign Policy places Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent — who has publicly warned Wall Street about frontier-model risk — and Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth among the principals, alongside the Commerce order from Secretary Howard Lutnick to CEO Dario Amodei. Officials had reportedly sought a voluntary suspension first, which Anthropic declined before the formal directive landed.

The reversal framing. Bloomberg and Insurance Journal read the shutoff as a hard pivot in US posture — from an administration that spent the spring promoting American AI to one reaching for a model-level kill switch, a "warning to Silicon Valley." Yet the government's stance is internally split: CDO Magazine reports the White House is weighing wider Mythos deployment inside federal agencies even while the public ban holds — the same capability judged too dangerous to sell is being evaluated for government use.

Who pulled the trigger. Axios and Fortune report Amazon called administration officials to share a report in which its researchers used a prompt chain to get Fable 5 to surface cyberattack-relevant information meant to be off-limits; calls from Amazon plus at least five other companies preceded the Friday-night shutdown. Semafor separately links the move to concern that a China-linked group had accessed Mythos — still uncorroborated by named officials and disputed by Anthropic.

Watch next. Whether the talks yield a restoration date or a narrowing from a global shutoff to nationality gating; whether the federal-expansion track is confirmed; whether the China-access claim is substantiated; and whether a parallel directive touches OpenAI or Google. Trump's Jun 2 AI executive order remains the backdrop, and commentators keep treating the episode as a stress test of a missing government AI-safety playbook (Tech Policy Press, Reason).
