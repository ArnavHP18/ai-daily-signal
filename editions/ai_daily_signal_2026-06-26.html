Anthropic swaps its negotiator and the odds flip — Fable 5 / Mythos 5 may be days from coming back

For two weeks the Anthropic export blackout was a stalemate. Today it moved. Anthropic replaced CEO Dario Amodei with co-founder and chief compute officer Tom Brown (the GPT-3 architect) at the Commerce Department table, alongside policy chief Sarah Heck, and the tone of the talks reportedly softened — enough that Polymarket's "Fable 5 back next week" contract jumped from ~15% to ~60% on the personnel change (AI Weekly, Globe and Mail). Yet the substance is unchanged: Anthropic confirms zero Fable/Mythos traffic is being served on Day 15 (explainx), no deal is signed, and the directive still bars every foreign national worldwide (Anthropic). The forcing function is the calendar: four bipartisan members of Congress demanded Commerce Secretary Lutnick explain the ban in writing by today, June 26; a reported government ID-verification milestone on July 8 is the likely US-first restoration path; and Kalshi still prices only ~57% for restoration before Jul 1.

So what: the market is pricing a thaw, not a resolution — keep the fallback live (open weights, GPT-5.5, your remaining Claude tiers), and assume any switch-back is US-first and nationality-gated rather than a clean global flip.

IMPACT 7/10#1 HARDWARE · OPENAI

OpenAI unveils its first custom chip — Jalapeño, built with Broadcom

OpenAI and Broadcom revealed Jalapeño, OpenAI's first "Intelligence Processor": a reticle-sized inference ASIC architected from scratch around OpenAI's LLM-serving roadmap, with Celestica on manufacturing (OpenAI, CNBC). It reached tape-out in ~nine months — a pace OpenAI credits partly to using its own models in the design loop — and early testing claims "substantially better" performance-per-watt than current state of the art. It's the first of a multi-generation platform targeting gigawatt-scale deployment from end-2026 (TechCrunch).

OpenAI is moving to own the inference layer, not just rent it — a structural bid to cut cost-per-token and NVIDIA dependence; watch whether "build your own accelerator" becomes table stakes for every frontier lab.

Gartner: AI-agent software spend hits ~$206.5B in 2026, +139% YoY

Gartner now forecasts ~$206.5B of AI-agent software spending in 2026 — up ~139% from $86.4B in 2025 — and ~$376.3B in 2027, the fastest-growing slice of enterprise software (Gartner via Itential, stats roundup).
