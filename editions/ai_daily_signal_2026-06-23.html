Day 12: the flagships stay dark as the Jul 1 clock nears — and allies float a "trusted partner" lane

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain disabled worldwide into Tuesday, twelve days after the Jun 12 Commerce directive barring foreign-national access. There is still no formal lifting and no signed deal; the directive stands. The talks grind on — Anthropic's technical staff have met with Commerce nearly daily and Secretary Howard Lutnick has held regular calls as officials "work toward a deal" (Globe and Mail). The fresher thread is structural: Bloomberg reports the crackdown claims a new federal power over deployed AI models, and G7 sources say allies have floated a "trusted partner" framework — restoring access for select allied nations or firms — though no agreement has been reached. The most concrete read stays the prediction markets: Kalshi traders put roughly 57% on access returning before Jul 1 (eight days out) and 67% before Jul 10 (Kalshi). Allies stay cut off; the ban "further strains alliances" (Al Jazeera).

So what: the rhetoric softened last week but nothing has been signed and the clock is now the story — with the Jul 1 market-implied window eight days out, keep your fallback live and migration priced, and assume any switch-back lands with nationality-gating or a "trusted partner" gate attached rather than a clean reversal.

Where it stands. As of this Tuesday scan, both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are still offline for all users worldwide — Day 12 of the blackout; every other Claude model remains available, with traffic routed to Opus 4.8. Anthropic continues to dispute the directive while complying, and says it is working to restore access "as soon as possible" (Fortune, Anthropic).

The deal track. Per the Globe and Mail, Anthropic's technical staff have met with Commerce officials nearly every day, and Secretary Lutnick has held regular calls as he "works toward a deal"; the government is seeking assurances the models cannot be turned against the US. No signed agreement and no restoration timeline have been confirmed — markets remain the most concrete read (Kalshi ~57% before Jul 1, ~67% before Jul 10; Kalshi, CNBC).

The new wrinkle: an institutionalized power, and a "trusted partner" idea. Bloomberg reports that in handling Anthropic, Lutnick's Commerce Department has effectively claimed a new authority to police deployed frontier models, not just chips — a precedent that outlasts this one case. Separately, G7 diplomatic sources say allied governments have raised a "trusted partner" scheme that would restore Fable 5 / Mythos 5 access to vetted allied nations or companies; nothing has been agreed, and the idea is reported, not confirmed by Commerce.

The policy frame. The shutoff followed a Jun 2 executive order, "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security," directing a secure-deployment framework with up to 30 days of voluntary government early access before wide release (CFR). The proximate trigger remains a jailbreak report; the on-record rationale is Lutnick's fear of diversion to military intelligence in China or Russia (US News). Last week Trump told The Axios Show he no longer views Anthropic as a threat — "not now, but a week ago, maybe" — but the directive was never lifted on the strength of that remark.

The alliance cost. Allies remain locked out: the ban "further strains alliances," with European and Asian partners unable to access Fable 5 or Mythos 5 while negotiations run, and the EU Commission has warned the curbs "should not be discriminatory" (Al Jazeera, Euronews). Anthropic spent the prior week opening a Seoul office and signing Samsung/LG/NAVER deployments in a market it cannot fully serve (today's #6).

Watch next. Whether a deal is signed inside the Jul 1 market-implied window; whether restoration arrives clean, nationality-gated, or via a "trusted partner" lane; and whether the new model-level authority Bloomberg describes gets pointed at any other lab.

The model-level export-control precedent hardens into a claimed federal power

The Jun 12 order is now an established precedent — and, per Bloomberg, an institutionalized one. Washington moved from restricting chips to suspending a deployed frontier model on national-security grounds, and has held it twelve days; Bloomberg reports that in doing so Lutnick's Commerce Department has effectively claimed a standing authority to police deployed AI models, a power that outlasts the Anthropic case. Trump's Axios remark that Anthropic is "not now" a threat (today's #1) eases the politics but does not undo the action; the directive remains in force. It sits atop the Jun 2 executive order "Promoting Advanced AI Innovation and Security," which set up a secure-deployment framework with up to 30 days of voluntary government early access (CFR). The reasoning is on the record: Commerce Secretary Lutnick said officials feared diversion to military-intelligence users in China, Russia or other countries of concern. Pushback persists: a 76-signatory open letter argues the models are "not uniquely good" and China's are "only months behind," and the EU Commission warned the curbs "should not be discriminatory" (Euronews). No parallel directive against OpenAI or Google has surfaced.

Every US frontier lab now has a worked example of a cabinet-level model suspension — and a stated claim that the power is permanent. War-game a national-security shutoff this quarter and document your nationality-gating capability regardless of how the Anthropic case ends.

IMPACT 6/10#10 POLICY · EU

EU clock: 40 days to Aug 2 transparency + GPAI enforcement

Near steady state, calendar ticking. From Aug 2 the AI Act brings Article 50 transparency duties (disclosure when users interact with AI; labelling of synthetic media) plus the start of the Commission's GPAI enforcement powers — documentation/information requests, model evaluations, compliance/risk-mitigation measures, and fines up to the greater of €15M or 3% of global turnover (Latham, artificialintelligenceact.eu). The GPAI Code of Practice (transparency, copyright, safety & security) is the practical compliance tool; signing carries a "presumption of conformity." The safety chapter binds only the ~5–15 systemic-risk providers above 10^25 FLOP; ~24 firms (Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, Mistral, OpenAI and others) have signed. A separate Digital Omnibus postponement of some high-risk obligations remains provisionally agreed, not yet formally adopted — Aug 2 transparency and GPAI enforcement still stand.

The Aug 2 enforcement switch is 40 days out — if your synthetic-media labelling and Article 50 transparency workstreams aren't staffed, they're late.
