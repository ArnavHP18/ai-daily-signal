Anthropic ships a cheaper agent — and it beats Opus where agents actually live

On June 30, in the same breath as the export ban lifting, Anthropic released Claude Sonnet 5 — pitched explicitly as "a cheaper way to run agents" (TechCrunch, Anthropic). Introductory pricing is $2 / $10 per Mtok through Aug 31 (then $3/$15), with a native 1M-token context. It trails the flagship Opus 4.8 on SWE-bench Pro (63.2% vs 69.2%) but beats it on Terminal-Bench 2.1 (80.4% vs 74.6%) and edges it on GDPval knowledge work — and it is now the default model for Free/Pro and in Claude Code. The framing is the story: enterprises "recoiled from agentic AI bills in Q2 2026 as tokenmaxxing burned through annual budgets in weeks," and Sonnet 5 is the direct answer.

So what: the frontier's centre of gravity is sliding from peak capability to cost-per-agent-run. A mid-tier model that beats the flagship on terminal/agent work at a fraction of the price rewrites the build calculus — re-benchmark your agent stack before Aug 31, because the price that keeps your token model viable is the temporary one.

The benchmark picture. Sonnet 5 is deliberately not the top of the stack — Opus 4.8 still wins SWE-bench Pro and OSWorld — but it wins where cost compounds: Terminal-Bench 2.1 (80.4% vs Opus's 74.6%), long-horizon agent loops, and knowledge work (GDPval-AA v2 ~1,618 Elo, edging Opus). Native 1M context lets it hold whole codebases in a single agent run.

The market read. Q2 2026 was the quarter enterprises discovered agentic coding can consume ~1,000× the tokens of single-turn chat. Anthropic's answer isn't a smaller context or a rate limit — it's a frontier-adjacent model at one-third to one-half the token price, defaulted on for everyone. Google is running the same play with Gemini 3.5 Flash; the "efficiency turn" is now the competitive axis.

IMPACT 6/10#3 POLICY · INDIA / JOBS (DEEP BEAT)

Saturday deep beat: India's GCCs scale a 5.1-lakh peak on AI skills

India's Global Capability Centres recorded 227,991 hires through June 2026 (+11% YoY), with foundit projecting full-year 2026 GCC hiring to hit a record 510,452 (+12%) (Business Today). Nearly two in three (64%) new GCC roles now require AI/data/automation skills, with AI/Data/Analytics the fastest-growing function at +38% YoY (Swarajya). A fresh Nasscom-Zinnov report (Jul 3) says India's GCCs are increasingly leading the AI mandate for global enterprises (Business Standard). Bengaluru holds a 30% share (~24,000 openings); Tier-2 cities grow fastest (+23%). Counter-signal: overall tech openings fell 8% YoY in July (Deccan Herald).

India's GCCs are moving up from staffing global back-offices to owning the AI mandate — but the 8% overall decline is the tell: entry without AI/data skills is closing fast.

IMPACT 4/10#10 POLICY · GLOBAL

The UN opens its Global Dialogue on AI Governance in Geneva

The UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance — established by UN General Assembly resolution — opens in Geneva on July 6, the first standing forum where member states discuss international approaches to managing AI (UN News). It arrives with a sharpened UN message that "the world needs to act now," and lands as national regimes diverge — the EU's Aug 2 enforcement, the US's voluntary managed-access EO, India's move toward a bespoke AI law. Early-stage and non-binding, but the first multilateral venue with the whole membership at the table.

Don't expect binding rules from Geneva — but a permanent UN venue is where interoperability (or fragmentation) between the EU/US/India/China regimes will first be negotiated. Multinationals should track it as the long-run harmonisation signal.
