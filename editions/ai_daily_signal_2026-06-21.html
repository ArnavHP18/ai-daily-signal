Day 10: the flagships stay dark — but Trump says Anthropic is "not now" a security threat

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain disabled worldwide into Sunday, ten days after the Jun 12 Commerce directive barring foreign-national access. The fresh signal is a striking rhetorical reversal: asked by The Axios Show whether he still saw Anthropic or Dario Amodei as a national-security risk, President Trump replied "Well, not now, but a week ago, maybe," crediting the company for responding "very quickly" and "responsibly" to the order (Benzinga, Storyboard18). The catch: the export directive is not formally lifted and the models are still off. Anthropic and Commerce remain "working toward a deal," and prediction markets still price the most concrete timeline — Kalshi traders put roughly 57% on access returning before Jul 1, 67% before Jul 10 (Kalshi). Allies are restless: the ban "further strains alliances," with EU and Asian partners cut off from a US lab's best models (Al Jazeera).

So what: the President softening the threat language is the clearest tailwind yet for restoration — but "not now" is not "lifted." Keep your fallback live and migration priced, and assume any switch-back still arrives with nationality-gating attached.

Where it stands. As of this Sunday scan, both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are still offline for all users worldwide — Day 10 of the blackout; every other Claude model remains available, with traffic routed to Opus 4.8. Anthropic continues to dispute the directive while complying, and says it is working to restore access "as soon as possible" (Fortune, Anthropic).

The new note: the President backs off the "threat" framing. In an interview with The Axios Show, Trump said his view of Anthropic had changed over the past week, that the company responded "very quickly" and "responsibly," and that he no longer regards it as a national-security threat — "not now, but a week ago, maybe" (Benzinga, The News). The remark caps a week of contact, including Trump's read of Amodei as "nice" and "smart" after the G7 in Évian-les-Bains. An Anthropic spokesperson said the company appreciated its "ongoing engagement" with the administration.

The deal track. Per the Globe and Mail, Anthropic's technical staff have met with Commerce officials nearly every day, and Secretary Lutnick has held regular calls as he "works toward a deal"; the government is seeking assurances the models cannot be turned against the US. No signed agreement and no restoration timeline have been confirmed — markets remain the most concrete read (Kalshi ~57% before Jul 1, ~67% before Jul 10; Kalshi, CNBC).

The policy frame. The shutoff followed a Jun 2 executive order, "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security," directing a secure-deployment framework with up to 30 days of voluntary government early access before wide release (CFR). The proximate trigger remains a jailbreak report; the on-record rationale is Lutnick's fear of diversion to military intelligence in China or Russia (US News).

The alliance cost. Even as Washington's tone softens, allies remain locked out: the ban "further strains alliances," with European and Asian partners unable to access Fable 5 or Mythos 5 while negotiations run, and the EU Commission has warned the curbs "should not be discriminatory" (Al Jazeera, Euronews). Anthropic spent the week opening a Seoul office and signing Samsung/LG/NAVER deployments in a market it cannot fully serve (today's #5).

Watch next. Whether the softened rhetoric converts into an actual restoration inside the Jul 1 market-implied window; whether it arrives clean or narrowed to nationality gating; whether a signed deal is announced; and whether any parallel directive touches OpenAI or Google.

IMPACT 6/10#10 POLICY · EU

EU clock: 42 days to Aug 2 transparency + GPAI enforcement

Near steady state, calendar ticking. From Aug 2 the AI Act brings Article 50 transparency duties (disclosure when users interact with AI; labelling of synthetic media) plus the start of the Commission's GPAI enforcement powers — documentation/information requests, model evaluations, compliance/risk-mitigation measures, and fines up to the greater of €15M or 3% of global turnover (Latham, artificialintelligenceact.eu). The GPAI Code of Practice (transparency, copyright, safety & security) is the practical compliance tool; signing carries a "presumption of conformity." The safety chapter binds only the ~5–15 systemic-risk providers above 10^25 FLOP; ~24 firms (Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, Mistral, OpenAI and others) have signed. A separate Digital Omnibus postponement of some high-risk obligations remains provisionally agreed, not yet formally adopted — Aug 2 transparency and GPAI enforcement still stand.

The Aug 2 enforcement switch is 42 days out — if your synthetic-media labelling and Article 50 transparency workstreams aren't staffed, they're late.
