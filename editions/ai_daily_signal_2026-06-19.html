Day 8: Anthropic's flagships stay dark — but an exec in Seoul says they'll be back "in the coming days"

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain disabled worldwide into Friday, eight days after the Jun 12 Commerce directive barring foreign-national access. The newest signal is the most optimistic yet — and the least concrete: at a Seoul press conference Wednesday, Anthropic's Managing Director of International, Chris Ciauri, said the company is "very confident that in the coming days, the models will become available again." It tracks with reporting that officials and Anthropic are working toward a deal, with Secretary Howard Lutnick on regular calls. But "coming days" is not a date, and there is still no signed agreement — the same on-record national-security rationale (feared diversion to military-intelligence users in China, Russia or other countries of concern) remains the basis for the shutoff. Ciauri also used the Seoul stage to name Korea a top-growth market and pledge a bigger local team.

So what: confidence from an executive is not a restoration date. Eight days in, treat "coming days" as guidance, not a guarantee — keep your fallback live and your migration priced, and expect any switch-back to arrive with nationality-gating attached.

Where it stands. As of this Friday scan, both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are still offline for all users worldwide — Day 8 of the blackout; every other Claude model remains available, with traffic routed to Opus 4.8. Anthropic continues to dispute the directive while complying, and says it is working to restore access "as soon as possible" (Fortune, Anthropic).

The new note: "coming days." Speaking at a Seoul press conference, international chief Chris Ciauri said, "We are very confident that in the coming days, the models will become available again" (Korea JoongAng Daily, Digg). It is the most upbeat framing from a named Anthropic executive since the shutoff — but it is a confidence statement, not a committed date, and no signed deal has been announced.

The deal track. Per the Globe and Mail, Anthropic's technical staff have met with Commerce officials nearly every day since the prior Friday, and Secretary Lutnick has held regular calls as he "works toward a deal"; the government is seeking assurances the models cannot be turned against the US. No agreement and no restoration timeline have been confirmed.

The rationale, on record. Commerce Secretary Lutnick said he acted because officials feared Fable 5 and Mythos 5 could be diverted to military-intelligence users in China, Russia or other countries of concern — the first named-official reason. The Amazon-jailbreak account (Axios) remains the proximate trigger; Anthropic disputes that a narrow potential jailbreak justifies recalling a model deployed to hundreds of millions.

The wider backdrop. At the Jun 17 G7 in Évian, Amodei and DeepMind's Hassabis called for a US-led AI coalition even as Amodei negotiated his own models back (CNBC); a 76-signatory security letter (Stamos, Moussouris) still presses for reversal, arguing the models are "not uniquely good" and China's are "only months behind" (Business Standard); and the EU Commission has warned the curbs "should not be discriminatory" (Euronews).

Watch next. Whether "coming days" produces an actual restoration this week, and whether it arrives clean or narrowed to nationality gating; whether a signed deal is announced; and whether any parallel directive touches OpenAI or Google. Commentators keep treating the episode as a live test of a missing government AI-safety playbook (Tech Policy Press).

IMPACT 6/10#9 POLICY · EU

EU clock: 44 days to Aug 2 transparency + GPAI enforcement

Near steady state, calendar ticking. From Aug 2 the AI Act brings Article 50 transparency duties (disclosure when users interact with AI; labelling of synthetic media) plus the start of the Commission's GPAI enforcement powers — documentation/information requests, model evaluations, compliance/risk-mitigation measures, and fines up to the greater of €15M or 3% of global turnover (Latham, artificialintelligenceact.eu). The GPAI Code of Practice (transparency, copyright, safety & security) is the practical compliance tool; signing carries a "presumption of conformity." The safety chapter binds only the ~5–15 systemic-risk providers above 10^25 FLOP; ~24 firms (Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, Mistral, OpenAI and others) have signed. A separate Digital Omnibus postponement of some high-risk obligations remains provisionally agreed, not yet formally adopted — Aug 2 transparency and GPAI enforcement still stand.

The Aug 2 enforcement switch is 44 days out — if your synthetic-media labelling and Article 50 transparency workstreams aren't staffed, they're late.
