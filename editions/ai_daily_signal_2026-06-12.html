SPCX priced at $135 — the largest IPO in history starts trading tonight

SpaceX's offering priced after Wednesday's US close exactly as structured: a fixed $135 per share, no range, for 555,555,555 Class A shares — a roughly $75 billion raise at a ~$1.75 trillion valuation, past Saudi Aramco's 2019 record. Demand blew through $250 billion, making the book roughly four times oversubscribed (Bloomberg), with retail orders alone topping $70 billion against an unusually large 30% retail reservation. The ticker SPCX goes live on Nasdaq today — the US open is ~19:00 IST tonight.

So what: tonight's first print is the cleanest read on public-market appetite for the entire 2026 AI-adjacent mega-listing pipeline — and, because the merged entity contains xAI, it is also the first public-market mark on a frontier AI lab. If SPCX holds above $135, OpenAI's September-at-earliest window firms up.

The deal. Final pricing landed Jun 11 after the US close at the fixed $135 the roadshow advertised — no bookbuild price discovery, a deliberate break with mega-IPO convention. 555.6M Class A shares for a ~$75B raise values the combined SpaceX–Starlink–xAI entity at roughly $1.75T (some outlets compute $1.77T on fully diluted counts). Trading begins today on Nasdaq Global Select and Nasdaq Texas under SPCX; the offering formally closes Jun 15.

The demand. Bloomberg reported the book more than four times oversubscribed before it closed; TradingKey puts total demand past $250B with some single institutional orders above $10B. Crypto Briefing tracked retail orders past $70B — itself ~3x the retail tranche, which at 30% of the float is triple-to-six-times the typical 5–10% retail allocation in deals this size.

The AI angle. Because xAI sits inside the merged company, tonight is the first time a frontier-AI lab carries a public-market price. Every private AI mark — xAI's own, Anthropic's $965B (May Series H), OpenAI's $852B (March) — gets an implicit public comp from how SPCX trades against the xAI sliver of its sum-of-parts.

The caution. Fixed-price mega-deals can break: TradingKey's analysts invoke Facebook's 2012 slide below its IPO price; Jim Cramer flagged the unusually heavy retail base as a stability risk (CNBC, Jun 10). Oversubscription at a fixed price tells you about allocation scarcity, not necessarily about where the stock settles.

Watch next. Tonight's open (~19:00 IST) and Monday's formal close of the offering; then whether the OpenAI S-1 thread (story #7) picks a date off the back of it.
