The US government switched off Anthropic's two best models — worldwide

At 5:21pm ET on Friday, Jun 12, the US Commerce Department handed Anthropic an export-control directive ordering it to cut off all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for any foreign national — inside or outside the US, including Anthropic's own foreign-national employees. Because the company says it cannot reliably sort users by nationality in real time, it complied the only way it could: disabling both models for everyone, everywhere. All of Anthropic's other models stay up. Fable 5 had been live for three days — it launched Jun 9. Anthropic disputes the rationale, is complying anyway, and says it is "working to restore access."

So what: this is the first time Washington has reached past chip controls to switch off a deployed US frontier model on national-security grounds. Every Anthropic-dependent team lost its top tier overnight; every other lab just learned the government holds a model-level kill switch. Audit your Fable/Mythos dependencies and stage a fallback today.

The directive. Per Axios, which broke the story, the Commerce Department — under Secretary Howard Lutnick — delivered the order to CEO Dario Amodei citing national-security authorities. It requires suspending all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 by "any foreign national, whether inside or outside the United States, including foreign national Anthropic employees." Bloomberg and CNBC confirm the contours.

Why it became a global blackout. Anthropic says it has no reliable way to gate access by nationality in real time, so to comply fully it took both models offline for its entire customer base worldwide. Access to every other Claude model is unaffected — but Fable 5 and Mythos 5, its two most capable systems, are simply gone for now (NBC News).

The rationale — and the dispute. The government's stated concern is a method of "jailbreaking" a safeguard meant to stop Fable 5 from being used to find software vulnerabilities; the technique reportedly amounts to asking the model to read a codebase and fix flaws. Anthropic counters that it has seen only verbal evidence of a narrow, non-universal jailbreak, and says it independently verified that rival models — including OpenAI's GPT-5.5 — can do the same task. Recalling a model deployed to hundreds of millions over that, the company argues, would "essentially halt all new model deployments for all frontier model providers" if applied industry-wide (Fortune).

The policy backdrop. The move follows the Jun 2 Trump executive order directing federal agencies to build a mechanism for the government to gain early access to the most powerful AI models on a voluntary basis. Friday's directive is the first hard exercise of that posture — a shift from controlling compute to controlling the models themselves.

Watch next. Anthropic said it would share more within 24 hours of the directive (i.e. today) and is working to restore access; no timeline is public. Watch for whether the carve-out narrows to foreign-national gating rather than a full shutoff, whether OpenAI or Google receive parallel orders, and any congressional reaction.
