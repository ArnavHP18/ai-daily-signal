Washington turned Anthropic's flagship back on: Fable 5 is live again worldwide after the export ban is lifted.

The 21-day standoff that has led this briefing since June 12 is over. The US Commerce Department / BIS withdrew the June 12 export-control order (dated ~Jun 30), and Anthropic began redeploying Fable 5 globally on July 1 across the Claude Platform, Claude.ai, Claude Code and Cowork; Mythos 5 — earlier cleared only for ~100 vetted US critical-infrastructure orgs — was cleared too (CNBC, Axios, Anthropic). In exchange Anthropic agreed to proactively detect and report security risks and help develop model-safety standards; it says it shipped an updated classifier that blocks the Amazon-reported jailbreak — the software-vulnerability finding that triggered the June 12 suspension — in >99% of cases, routing blocked prompts to Opus 4.8. The restoration is staged, not instantaneous: Fable 5 counts toward up to ~50% of weekly usage limits through Jul 7 for Pro/Max/Team, and a separate government-ID / age-verification gate (via vendor Persona) begins Jul 8 for consumer plans (API/Team/Enterprise exempt).

So what: the acute crisis is resolved, but the precedent isn't. A cabinet-level, model-specific on/off switch was used and then released in three weeks — build that contingency into every frontier-dependent plan, keep the open-weight fallback you qualified during the blackout, and read the Jul 8 ID gate as the new cost of consumer access.

What changed. On June 12 the administration invoked export-control authority to switch off Anthropic's two most capable models — Fable 5 (the general-purpose flagship millions used) and Mythos 5 (its strongest cyber model) — worldwide, after an Amazon researcher's report of a jailbreak that surfaced software vulnerabilities. A June 26 letter first restored Mythos 5 for ~100 Annex-A US critical-infrastructure organizations (Project Glasswing). Then, around June 30, Commerce withdrew the order entirely; Anthropic re-enabled Fable 5 for all users starting July 1 (CNBC, CNN Business, Al Jazeera).

The terms. Reporting describes reciprocal commitments: Anthropic will give the government proactive security testing and reporting, contribute to model-safety standards, and has trained a classifier that it says blocks the specific jailbreak in >99% of cases (redirecting to Opus 4.8). Consumer access now carries an identity cost — the Jul 8 Persona government-ID / age check, first flagged in late June, proceeds on schedule; API and enterprise customers are exempt.

Why it's the lead. This is the decisive event the feed has tracked for 22 arc-days — a resolution, not another holding pattern — and it resets the strategic picture: the immediate export exposure on Anthropic is gone, but the June 2 EO's voluntary early-access framework (Developing #2) and the EU's binding Aug 2 clock (Developing #3) still define the regime the industry now ships under.

Caveats & watch next. The restoration is staged (50% weekly-usage weighting through Jul 7) and details rest on trade/business press plus Anthropic's own posts (anthropic.com returned 403 to our fetcher; cross-checked via CNBC/Axios/CNN/NBC). IMPACT 7/10#2 SECURITY · AGENTS

"DuneSlide": two CVSS 9.8 zero-click RCE flaws in the Cursor IDE

Cato AI Labs disclosed CVE-2026-50548 and CVE-2026-50549 — sandbox-escape bugs in Cursor's run_terminal_cmd tool that let a single injected instruction in an MCP response or web-search result escape the terminal sandbox and run arbitrary code with no click. Patched in Cursor 3.0 (Apr 2); every prior version is affected; Cursor's maker says >half the Fortune 500 use it (The Hacker News, Cato Networks, CSO Online).

Prompt injection is now a first-class RCE vector against dev tools, not just chatbots — force-upgrade Cursor to ≥3.0, audit which MCP servers and web-search tools your agents can reach, and treat tool output as untrusted input.

WHAT CHANGED De-escalated: the acute model-suspension is over, but the structural regime persists — the Jun 2 EO still directs agencies to design a voluntary frontier early-access framework (up to ~30 days pre-release access, "trusted partners"; no mandatory licensing) by ~Aug 1. The crisis is over but the rulebook isn't — build pre-release federal review and partner-gating into launch plans, and read the Aug 1 framework design as the shape of things to come.
