Day 9: Anthropic's flagships stay dark — now prediction markets, not executives, set the clock

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain disabled worldwide into Saturday, nine days after the Jun 12 Commerce directive barring foreign-national access — no overnight restoration despite Wednesday's "coming days" line from international chief Chris Ciauri. With no signed deal to point to, the freshest read is from the betting markets: Kalshi traders now price roughly 57% that access returns before Jul 1 and 67% before Jul 10 (CNBC, Kalshi). Today, Jun 20, is also the refund deadline for subscribers who paid for Fable 5 between Jun 9–14. Anthropic and Commerce officials are still "working toward a deal," with Secretary Lutnick on regular calls; no agreement, and the same national-security rationale (feared diversion to military-intelligence users in China, Russia or other countries of concern) still governs. In a striking split-screen, Anthropic spent the week opening a Seoul office and signing Samsung/LG/NAVER deployments — in a market its best models can't currently serve.

So what: nine days in, the restoration is a probability, not a promise — markets say more-likely-than-not by Jul 1, but far from certain. Keep your fallback live and your migration priced, and assume any switch-back arrives with nationality-gating attached.

Where it stands. As of this Saturday scan, both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are still offline for all users worldwide — Day 9 of the blackout; every other Claude model remains available, with traffic routed to Opus 4.8. Anthropic continues to dispute the directive while complying, and says it is working to restore access "as soon as possible" (Fortune, Anthropic).

The new note: markets put a number on it. Wednesday's "very confident…in the coming days" from international chief Chris Ciauri (Korea JoongAng Daily) has not produced a restoration. With no date attached, prediction markets are now the most concrete signal: Kalshi traders price roughly a 57% chance access is restored before Jul 1 and 67% before Jul 10 (CNBC, Kalshi) — more-likely-than-not, but materially short of certain.

The deal track. Per the Globe and Mail, Anthropic's technical staff have met with Commerce officials nearly every day, and Secretary Lutnick has held regular calls as he "works toward a deal"; the government is seeking assurances the models cannot be turned against the US. No agreement and no restoration timeline have been confirmed.

The policy frame. The shutoff followed a Jun 2 executive order, "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security," directing a secure-deployment framework with up to 30 days of voluntary government early access before wide release (CFR). The proximate trigger remains Amazon's jailbreak report; the on-record rationale is Lutnick's fear of diversion to military intelligence in China or Russia (US News).

The split-screen. Even as its top models stay dark, Anthropic opened a Seoul office on Jun 17 and announced enterprise deployments with Samsung SDS, LG CNS, NAVER, Nexon, Hanwha and Channel Corp (Anthropic) — committing to a market it cannot fully serve while the blackout holds (today's #4). A 76-signatory security letter still presses for reversal (Business Standard), and the EU Commission has warned the curbs "should not be discriminatory" (Euronews).

Watch next. Whether the Jul 1 market-implied window produces an actual restoration; whether it arrives clean or narrowed to nationality gating; whether a signed deal is announced; and whether any parallel directive touches OpenAI or Google.

IMPACT 8/10#3 POLICY · US

The model-level export-control precedent stands — refund clock runs while restoration is debated

The Jun 12 order is now an established precedent, whether or not access returns this week. Washington moved from restricting chips to suspending a deployed frontier model on national-security grounds — and has held it nine days. No parallel directive against OpenAI or Google has surfaced.

Every US frontier lab now has a worked example of a cabinet-level suspension with a stated military-diversion rationale — war-game a national-security shutoff this quarter and document your nationality-gating capability regardless of how the Anthropic case ends.

IMPACT 6/10#9 POLICY · EU

EU clock: 43 days to Aug 2 transparency + GPAI enforcement

Near steady state, calendar ticking. From Aug 2 the AI Act brings Article 50 transparency duties (disclosure when users interact with AI; labelling of synthetic media) plus the start of the Commission's GPAI enforcement powers — documentation/information requests, model evaluations, compliance/risk-mitigation measures, and fines up to the greater of €15M or 3% of global turnover (Latham, artificialintelligenceact.eu). The GPAI Code of Practice (transparency, copyright, safety & security) is the practical compliance tool; signing carries a "presumption of conformity." The safety chapter binds only the ~5–15 systemic-risk providers above 10^25 FLOP; ~24 firms (Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, Mistral, OpenAI and others) have signed. A separate Digital Omnibus postponement of some high-risk obligations remains provisionally agreed, not yet formally adopted — Aug 2 transparency and GPAI enforcement still stand.

The Aug 2 enforcement switch is 43 days out — if your synthetic-media labelling and Article 50 transparency workstreams aren't staffed, they're late.
