AI turns on the chip shortage itself — CuspAI raises $450M and builds a materials foundry with Nvidia and Samsung inside

On a quieter Tuesday the biggest genuinely-new item is a research bet with an industrial coalition behind it. CuspAI, a Cambridge (UK) startup using AI to discover new materials, raised a $450M Series B at a $2.6B valuation — up from ~$520M last September — led by Kleiner Perkins and NEA, with Bezos Expeditions, Lux Capital, AMD Ventures and Britain's Sovereign AI Venture Fund (Quartz, CuspAI, eWeek). Alongside it the company launched the AI Materials Foundry — a coalition of 45+ organizations including Nvidia, Meta, Samsung, Hyundai Motor Group and Lam Research — pooling compute and lab access. Semiconductors get 80% of CuspAI's research bandwidth this year, including work to remove supply-constrained rare metals (ruthenium, iridium) from chipmaking (Invezz).

So what: the same compute crunch driving the AI-capex fight (Developing D1) is being attacked from the materials layer — and the chip supply chain itself (Nvidia, Samsung, Lam) is co-investing. AI-for-science graduates from protein structure to the physical inputs of the chips it runs on.

ARC: AI-MATERIALS · D12x SRC
Quartz · CuspAI (primary) · eWeek · New Electronics · Invezz

What's new. CuspAI is a 2024-founded materials-discovery lab (co-founded with input from DeepMind/Meta AI alumni) that trains generative models to propose novel compounds — MOFs for carbon capture, then a hard pivot into semiconductor materials. The Jul 20 round roughly 5x's its valuation in ten months and is paired with the AI Materials Foundry, a members' coalition pooling GPU compute, wet-lab access and domain expertise on CuspAI's platform. Lab operations expand in Cambridge, Singapore and the SF Bay Area.

Why it leads today. No frontier model shipped on Jul 20–21; the cleanest, best-sourced new development is this. It is also the day's strongest research beat — AI-for-science aimed squarely at the industry's own bottleneck (advanced-node materials and rare-metal dependence), with the chipmakers as backers rather than customers.

The caveats. Materials-discovery claims are notoriously hard to validate: a generated candidate is a hypothesis until synthesized and characterized, and prior AI-materials waves (e.g. GNoME) drew scrutiny over how many "novel" structures were truly useful. The 80%-on-semiconductors framing and the "remove ruthenium/iridium" goal are the company's, not yet peer-reviewed results. Foundry membership is a commitment to collaborate, not disclosed capital.

Watch next. First Foundry deliverables and any peer-reviewed characterization; whether the semiconductor-materials angle intersects TSMC/Samsung roadmaps (Tracking: compute-supply, custom-silicon); and whether the UK Sovereign AI Fund's stake becomes a template for state-backed applied-science bets (Tracking: sovereign-ai).

IMPACT 6/10#3 POLICY · EU / ANTITRUST

EU orders Google to open Android's AI-assistant layer to rivals and share Search data

The European Commission adopted two binding DMA decisions (Jul 16) requiring Google to give rival AI assistants the same system-level Android access it reserves for Gemini — wake words, home button, screen reading, in-app actions — and to share Search data with rival engines (European Commission, TechTimes, Unite.AI). Search-data sharing begins Jan 2027; Android interop reaches users by Jul 2027; Google can appeal.

The first time the DMA is used to pry open the OS-level AI-assistant slot — a distribution lever that could shape Gemini's reach more than any benchmark. A template regulators (and India's MeitY) may copy.
