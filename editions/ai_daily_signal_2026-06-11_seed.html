Anthropic splits the frontier: Fable 5 goes public, Mythos 5 stays behind glass

Anthropic on June 9 released Claude Fable 5, the first generally available model in its new Mythos-class tier above Opus. Fable 5 leads on software engineering, knowledge work and vision — but enforces hard safety limits in cybersecurity, biology, chemistry and model-distillation domains, where it blocks and falls back to Claude Opus 4.8. The unrestricted twin, Claude Mythos 5, is limited to vetted organizations via Project Glasswing, which expanded to roughly 150 new organizations alongside the launch.

So what: a two-track frontier — open capability with classifiers vs. gated raw capability — is now a live commercial model, and Congress noticed: Rep. Trahan joined the Obernolte preemption bill citing concern about Mythos. Model-selection, procurement and policy teams all have homework this week.

What shipped. Claude Fable 5 launched June 9 across Anthropic's surfaces. Per TechCrunch, it is "a version of Mythos the public can access today," released days after Anthropic's own warnings that frontier AI is becoming dangerous in narrow domains. In flagged high-risk areas the model refuses and routes to Claude Opus 4.8 rather than answering.

The gated twin. Claude Mythos 5 shares the same underlying capabilities without the safety classifiers and is distributed only through Project Glasswing, Anthropic's vetted-access program, which expanded to ~150 additional organizations and added a "Claude Security" codebase-scanning offering at launch.

Business backdrop. The launch lands two weeks after Anthropic's $65B Series H at a $965B post-money valuation (May 28 — CNBC), which made it the most valuable private AI company, above OpenAI, on a reported ~$47B run-rate.

Policy ripple. The model is already shaping legislation: per the Christian Science Monitor, Rep. Lori Trahan (D-MA) joined Rep. Jay Obernolte's (R-CA) AI bill after expressing concern about Mythos; the bill centers on pre-release review of frontier models.

Watch next. Competitive responses are queued: Google has confirmed Gemini 3.5 Pro for June, and Grok 5 is rumored (unconfirmed) for later this month. Expect rapid benchmark and pricing comparisons against Fable 5 within days.

EU publishes final Code of Practice for marking and labelling AI-generated content

The Commission's final voluntary Code (Jun 10) operationalizes AI Act Article 50: machine-readable marking for AI audio/image/video/text, plus a standard icon set for deepfakes, fully-AI and partially-AI content. Transparency obligations apply from Aug 2, 2026; the content-labelling piece has a deferral to Dec 2, 2026.

If you generate or distribute synthetic content touching the EU, this Code is the de facto compliance playbook — adopting it early is cheaper than retrofitting in November.
