Day 3 of the blackout: Anthropic's two best models are still off — and now we know why

Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain disabled worldwide into Sunday, three days after the Jun 12 (5:21pm ET) US Commerce directive that barred foreign-national access to both. The weekend filled in the rationale: Semafor reports the White House acted partly over suspicion that a China-linked group had accessed Mythos, and a separate account describes a roughly 90-minute ultimatum to restrict access or face consequences — which is why Anthropic, unable to gate by nationality in real time, pulled both models entirely. Anthropic still calls it "a misunderstanding" and says it is "working to restore access," with no timeline public. A political backlash is now forming.

So what: the story has moved from "what happened" to "why" — and a China-access allegation makes a fast, clean reinstatement less likely. If you depend on Fable/Mythos, treat the outage as multi-day, not multi-hour: keep your fallback live and re-confirm it covers your highest-tier workloads.

Where it stands. As of this Sunday scan, both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are still offline for all users worldwide; every other Claude model remains available. Anthropic has not announced a restoration date and continues to dispute the directive while complying with it (Fortune, Anthropic).

The new "why." Semafor reports the administration's move was linked in part to concerns that a China-linked group had gained access to Mythos. An administration official told Axios that Commerce acted after another company claimed it could jailbreak Mythos, alarming officials about national-security exposure. A separate report describes a roughly 90-minute window Anthropic was given to restrict access before the order took effect.

The backlash. Policy voices reacted with alarm. Brad Carson, president of Americans for Responsible Innovation, said the government must be able to block advanced models but that "the utmost care is required to insulate these decisions from impulse and political favoritism," warning the call "risks America's edge in AI innovation" (TIME). AI critic Gary Marcus argued that cutting Chinese-born researchers off from frontier models could push talent back toward China — a self-defeating outcome.

Anthropic's defense, unchanged. The company maintains it has seen only verbal evidence of a narrow, non-universal jailbreak, that comparable capability exists in rival public models (it has named OpenAI's GPT-5.5), and that recalling a model deployed at scale on that basis "would essentially halt all new model deployments for all frontier model providers" if generalized across the industry.

Watch next. Whether the order narrows from a global shutoff to nationality gating; whether any China-access claim is substantiated or rebutted; whether a parallel directive touches OpenAI or Google; and whether Congress schedules oversight. The Jun 2 Trump AI executive order — directing agencies to build an early-access mechanism for frontier models — remains the policy backdrop.
