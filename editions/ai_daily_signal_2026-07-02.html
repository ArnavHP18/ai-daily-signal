After 19 days dark, Washington lifts the controls — Fable 5 is back worldwide, and Anthropic ships Sonnet 5.

The saga that led this feed since June 12 has broken open. The US government lifted the export controls on Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 (~Jun 30), and Fable 5 returned globally on July 1 across the Claude platform, Claude.ai, Claude Code and Cowork; Mythos 5 — the less-restricted cyber variant — is being reintroduced only to approved US organizations following government review (Anthropic, CoinDesk, AI Business). Anthropic says it shipped an updated automated classifier to patch the safeguard-bypass (the Amazon-found jailbreak that surfaced vulnerabilities and triggered the June-12 controls), clearing the path back. On the same beat it launched Claude Sonnet 5 (Jun 30) — now the default model for Free and Pro users from July 1 (New #1).

So what: the crisis is resolving, but on the government's terms — a tiered restoration, not a clean switch-back (Mythos 5 stays gated to vetted US orgs). Requalify Fable 5 in your workflows, but keep the export-aware access controls you built during the blackout: the authorization lane that gated these models is now the permanent shape of frontier releases, not a one-off.

How we got here. On June 12 the US applied export controls to Anthropic's two newest models after a report that Amazon researchers had bypassed Fable 5's safeguards to enumerate software vulnerabilities. For 19 days Fable 5 — a general-purpose flagship millions used — was suspended worldwide, and this feed tracked the standoff day by day, through a partial Mythos 5 clearance for ~100 vetted critical-infrastructure orgs (Jun 26) up to yesterday's "Day 19, still dark."

What changed. Commerce lifted the controls (~Jun 30). Fable 5 came back for everyone on July 1; Anthropic credits an updated automated classifier that patches the safeguard-bypass. Mythos 5 returns only to government-approved US organizations. For Pro, Max, Team and select Enterprise plans, Fable 5 is included for up to 50% of weekly usage limits through July 7, after which it draws on usage credits (Anthropic, AI Business).

Why it's the lead. This is a decisive, tier-changing event on the single biggest thread in this feed — a de-escalation, not just another day of the standoff. It also resets the models story: Sonnet 5 lands the same week (New #1), Claude reaches GA on Microsoft's Azure Foundry on NVIDIA GB300 (New #3), and the release rides the government-authorization framework that the whole June saga established (Developing #2).

Caveats & watch next. Mythos 5 access remains gated and export-licensed for non-approved parties; questions linger on how banks and other regulated buyers re-onboard the model (American Banker). Watch whether Anthropic publishes the classifier's efficacy data, whether the ~30-day pre-release review becomes a standing requirement, and whether the same lane is applied to the next OpenAI/Google flagship.

The authorization lane the saga leaves behind

WHAT CHANGED The acute episode ended — Commerce reversed the June-12 controls on Anthropic's models (~Jun 30), the first full rollback. But the Jun 2 EO's authorization framework persists as the default frontier-release path: Mythos 5 stays gated to approved US orgs, and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 (Sol/Terra/Luna) still ships under limited, government-approved access.
The Jun 2 EO asked labs to give the federal government up to ~30 days of pre-release access to covered frontier models; that lane governed both the Anthropic suspension/restoration and the GPT-5.6 gated preview. The reversal shows the regime can release and restrict — the default is now managed access, not open launch.

Build federal pre-release review and partner-gating into launch plans; the "authorized access" default outlasts any single blackout.
